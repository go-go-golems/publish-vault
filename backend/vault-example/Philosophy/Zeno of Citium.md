# Zeno of Citium

Zeno of Citium (c. 334 – c. 262 BC) was a Hellenistic philosopher from Citium, Cyprus. He was the founder of the [[Stoicism|Stoic school]] of philosophy, which he taught in Athens from about 300 BC.

## Life

Zeno came to Athens after a shipwreck and became a student of the Cynic philosopher Crates of Thebes. He later studied under other philosophers before founding his own school.

He taught in the *Stoa Poikile* (Painted Porch), which gave Stoicism its name.

## Contributions

- Founded the Stoic school of philosophy
- Developed the Stoic theory of logic
- Established the doctrine that virtue is the only good
