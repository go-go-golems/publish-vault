# Epistemology

Epistemology is the branch of philosophy concerned with the theory of knowledge. It studies the nature, origin, and scope of knowledge, as well as the rationality of belief.

## Central Questions

- What is knowledge?
- How is knowledge acquired?
- What do people know?
- What are the limits of knowledge?

## Justified True Belief

The classical definition of knowledge, attributed to Plato, is **justified true belief** (JTB):

> A person S knows that P if and only if:
> 1. P is true
> 2. S believes that P
> 3. S is justified in believing that P

This definition was challenged by Edmund Gettier in 1963 with his famous [[Gettier Problem]] counterexamples.

## Types of Knowledge

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| A priori | Independent of experience | Mathematical truths |
| A posteriori | Dependent on experience | Empirical facts |
| Propositional | "Knowing that" | Knowing that Paris is in France |
| Procedural | "Knowing how" | Knowing how to ride a bike |

## Relationship to Stoicism

[[Stoicism]] places great emphasis on reason as the path to virtue. The Stoics held that the *logos* — universal reason — is accessible through careful observation and rational thought.
