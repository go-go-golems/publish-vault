# Personal Knowledge Management

Personal Knowledge Management (PKM) is the practice of deliberately capturing, organising, and connecting information to support thinking, learning, and creative work. It is applied [[Philosophy/Epistemology|epistemology]] for the individual.

## The Problem PKM Solves

The modern knowledge worker is drowning in information. Articles, books, podcasts, conversations, meetings — the raw material of thought arrives faster than it can be processed. Without a system, most of it is lost. With a bad system, it is stored but never used. With a good system, it *compounds*.

> "We are not building a collection of information. We are building a second brain." — Tiago Forte

## Core Frameworks

### PARA (Tiago Forte)

PARA organises information by *actionability*:

- **Projects** — things with a deadline and a goal
- **Areas** — ongoing responsibilities (health, finances, relationships)
- **Resources** — topics of interest for future reference
- **Archives** — inactive items from the other three

### Zettelkasten (Luhmann)

See [[The Zettelkasten Method]] for a full treatment. The key difference from PARA: Zettelkasten organises by *connection* rather than category. Notes are linked to each other, not filed in folders.

### CODE (Forte)

The workflow that feeds PARA:

1. **Capture** — save anything that resonates
2. **Organise** — sort into PARA
3. **Distil** — highlight and summarise
4. **Express** — create something from your notes

## The Capture Habit

The most important PKM habit is **frictionless capture**. If capturing a thought requires more than five seconds, you will not do it consistently. The tool matters less than the habit.

```
Capture workflow:
  1. Thought / idea / quote arises
  2. Capture immediately (phone, paper, inbox note)
  3. Process during a weekly review
  4. Promote to permanent note or discard
```

## The Weekly Review

Without a regular review, your capture system becomes a graveyard. The weekly review is the maintenance ritual:

- [ ] Clear your capture inbox
- [ ] Process fleeting notes into permanent notes or discard
- [ ] Update project status
- [ ] Identify what to work on next week

## Relationship to Writing

A mature PKM system transforms writing from a blank-page problem into an assembly problem. See [[The Zettelkasten Method]], [[How to Write Clearly]], and [[The Writing Process]].

## Relationship to Philosophy

PKM is not just a productivity technique — it is a stance toward knowledge. The question "how should I organise my notes?" is downstream of the question "what is knowledge for?" See [[Philosophy/Epistemology|Epistemology]] and [[Philosophy/The Examined Life|The Examined Life]].
